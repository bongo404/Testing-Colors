Why you should use the Godot Engine

📅 October 3, 2025 👤 bongo404 ⏱️ 8 min read

In the ever-evolving landscape of game development, choosing the right engine can make or break your project. While Unity and Unreal Engine dominate the conversation, there's a powerful contender that's been quietly winning over developers: the Godot Engine. Today, we'll explore why this open-source powerhouse deserves your attention.

Open Source Freedom

Unlike proprietary engines that come with licensing fees and restrictions, Godot is completely free and open source under the MIT license. This means:

  • No royalties on your game sales - keep 100% of your revenue
  • Full access to the engine's source code for customization
  • Community-driven development with transparent roadmaps
  • No risk of the engine being discontinued or going behind a paywall
💡 Fun Fact: Godot has over 1,500 contributors and continues to grow, ensuring long-term support and constant improvements.

Lightweight & Fast

Godot's engine is remarkably lightweight, with the entire editor being less than 50MB. This makes it perfect for developers who:

  • Work on laptops or machines with limited resources
  • Need quick iteration times without long loading screens
  • Want to version control their entire project easily
  • Value simplicity and a clean workspace

GDScript: Python-like Simplicity

Godot's native scripting language, GDScript, is designed specifically for game development. It's syntactically similar to Python, making it incredibly easy to learn:

extends Node2D func _ready(): print("Hello, Godot!") $Sprite.position = Vector2(100, 100) func _process(delta): rotation += delta * 2.0

The language is tightly integrated with the engine, offering:

  • Fast compilation and execution
  • Built-in editor with syntax highlighting and auto-completion
  • Type hints for better error checking
  • No build step required - iterate quickly
🎯 Bonus: If you prefer, Godot also supports C#, C++, and various community-created language bindings!

Best-in-Class 2D and Growing 3D

Godot shines brightest in 2D game development. Its dedicated 2D engine (not just a 3D engine with orthographic camera) provides:

  • Pixel-perfect rendering
  • Intuitive animation system
  • Powerful tilemap editor
  • Built-in physics engine optimized for 2D

While historically weaker in 3D, Godot 4.0+ has made massive strides with Vulkan rendering, bringing it much closer to industry standards. The 3D workflow now includes:

  • Modern PBR materials and lighting
  • Global illumination and real-time reflections
  • Improved performance and optimization tools
  • Better import pipeline for 3D assets

The Verdict

Godot Engine represents a refreshing approach to game development: powerful, accessible, and truly free. Whether you're an indie developer working on your first game or an experienced studio looking for alternatives, Godot offers a compelling package.

The engine's commitment to open-source principles, combined with its growing feature set and passionate community, makes it an excellent choice for 2025 and beyond. The best part? You can download it right now and start creating without worrying about costs, contracts, or corporate control.

🚀 Ready to get started? Visit godotengine.org to download the engine and explore the extensive documentation!
